Why we exist

1.4 Billion People. 1 Open Ecosystem.

Mainstream AI systems under–represent Indic languages. IndicVerse is a public effort to collect datasets, train models, and release tools that speak and listen natively.

IndicVerse is dedicated to advancing natural language processing for Indic languages, especially low–resource ones that are often ignored by mainstream AI.

We bridge the gap by creating high–quality datasets, pre–trained models, and tools tailored to diverse Indic linguistic needs – and releasing them openly for the community.

  • Datasets: Indic NLP datasets for translation, classification, and generation.
  • Models: State‑of‑the‑art Indic models using PEFT, LoRA, and modern fine‑tuning.
  • Research: Work on transliteration, low‑resource learning, and domain‑specific Indic NLP.

What we build

Core technical pillars

From raw audio to aligned language models, IndicVerse focuses on three pillars that turn India’s speech into open infrastructure.

Featured projects

Hellaswag‑Telugu for advanced commonsense evaluation, and custom translation / mixed transliteration tools and APIs for Telugu‑English and other Indic pairs.

In progress
TTS · Speak

Text–to–Speech (TTS)

High–quality, locally flavoured voices that can read Indic text naturally – not as an afterthought.

Multi–speaker, multi–language TTS models with open licenses and tools for fine–tuning.

Active
STT · Listen

Speech–to–Text (STT)

Robust recognition for accents, code–mixing, and noisy real–world audio from Indian environments.

Datasets and baselines that let builders plug Indic speech into any product.

Next
LLMs · Understand

Indic Language Models

Fine–tuned open–source LLMs that read and write Indian languages with cultural and linguistic context.

Aligned for civic, educational, and developer use – not locked behind proprietary APIs.

Where we’re headed

IndicVerse roadmap

We are building in the open – from community to datasets to production–ready speech and language models.

Phase 1
The foundation

Community & datasets

Organising contributors, defining data standards, and curating open speech and text corpora across Indic languages.

Phase 2
The voice

Indic TTS / STT models

Training and releasing the first wave of open Indic TTS and STT models with recipes, benchmarks, and demos.

Phase 3
The mind

Native Indic LLMs

Fine–tuning, aligning, and serving Indic LLMs that understand local context, not just translate from English.
